Quantum particle statistics dictates the behavior of identical quantum particles, categorized into two types: bosonic and fermionic. This classification is established through either (anti)commutation constraints on creation and annihilation operators or the symmetrization postulate. Nevertheless, the foundational understanding of this particle-type duality remains elusive, triggering continuous debate since the advent of quantum mechanics. This workshop aims to bring together experts from quantum foundations, quantum information, mathematical physics, and philosophy of physics to share their perspectives and discuss the latest advancements in understanding the foundations of quantum particle statistics.
